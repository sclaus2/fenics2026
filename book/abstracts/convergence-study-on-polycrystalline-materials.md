**Submission type:** Presentation

**Presenter:** Donald Boyce (Cornell University)

Polycrystalline materials possess distinct material properties at the
individual grain scale, creating jumps (discontinuities) in properties
at grain boundaries. This leads to limitations in solution regularity
and global convergence rates of finite element calculations. This
study examines convergence rates on a grain-by-grain basis, focusing
on the convergence of grain-averaged quantities. We utilize conductive
heat transfer and linear elasticity as model problems to investigate
the relationship between relative errors and grain size.
